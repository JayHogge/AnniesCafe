Copyright Rules

Copyright is a fickle thing, it feels to the internet renegades and underprivileged user like police tape that one should not cross lest one risk lawsuits and cease and desist letters. Copyright, however, is an important part of the creative domain and ensures that creators, people who've taken the risky and highly competitive road into artistry, get a return on the value they've contributed to the cultural song. Many forms of copyright exist, but Creative Commons is one worth mentioning. Unlike Commercial copyrights, under the Creative Commons licensing, others are authorized to alter, modify, and be inspired by the licensed work and so long as copies of the work aren't being sold or being one-for-one redistributed under a different license to generate revenue, the work is yours to do with as you please.

Accessibility

Accessibility isn't something I had considered much before this class. Less-abled and disabled individuals surely want to participate in the experience which is the internet, and desiging websites to accomodate those people is an important consideration that developers need to make. On one hand, in many cases it may feel like a niche subset of users not worth catering to, but on the other, these people are just as interested and valued of users or customers as a fully-able person. Therefore, using 'alt' tags which can be used by text-to-speech software to describe photos for the blind is a great and easy accomadation a website can have.

Ethical Design

Creating a website that is ethically minded is something many engineer-types may disregard. The internet is the wild-west and very free and loose compared to sovereign countries, and ethical design isn't something many consider when they're far from the reaches of authority or legal consequence. However, ethical desiging is still something even criminals should consider, as their contributions to the internet shape the future of the net. Ethically creating a site includes transmitting data in an encrypted form, writing disclaimers of cookie and tracking technology use, and securing servers from abuse and easy-access by unauthorized individuals. It's necessary to create avenues where crime is not easily conducted.
